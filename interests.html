Data Stories I Enjoy

What I am exploring right now

I am learning how to communicate with data in a way that feels useful and clear. I like to focus on three parts of a project: gathering high quality data, building transparent models, and designing visuals that help people make decisions. Recently, I have been revisiting fundamentals like feature engineering, baselines, and careful evaluation along with more advanced ideas like prompt engineering and error analysis for NLP.

I also like blending this work with topics I enjoy outside of class. For example, I have been tracking fashion trends across social platforms, cleaning and aggregating engagement data, and building small dashboards to understand how tastes change. I am experimenting with reusable components so I can structure projects cleanly and share results with friends. My goal is to produce work that is honest about uncertainty and still practical.

Topics on my list

  • Visualization layouts that make comparisons easier
  • Fast data wrangling and reliable pipelines
  • Model evaluation with human centered error checks
  • Lightweight dashboards for non technical audiences
